making a zine

This guide walks you through making a zine in zine machine, from opening the tool to folding the finished pages. Each step shows the button to press, circled in yellow. If you’re working in a group, start at step 1; if you’re on your own, jump to step 3.

1join your group

If you’re working in a group, this is how you get into the same shared zine. (Working alone? Skip to step 3.)

2divide up the pages

Everyone with the same code edits one zine together, live. Decide who takes which page so you don’t overwrite each other.

3pick a format

Tell the tool how your zine will be built and what paper you’ll print on.

4load an example (optional)

The fastest way to learn the tool is to look at a finished one.

5design your pages

This is where you make your zine — words, pictures, and color, one page at a time.

6preview your zine

Check how it looks and how it’ll print before you commit paper to it.

7print your zine

Turn your pages into a printable file.

8fold & cut

The last step happens off-screen, with your hands.

a few good habits

  • In a group? Talk to each other. Claim a page (“I’ve got the cover”) so two people aren’t editing the same one at once.
  • Your work saves automatically — in a group, to your shared zine; solo, use “save project” to keep a copy you can re-open later.
  • Preview before you print. It’s the easiest way to catch a page that’s upside down or empty.
  • “Print the pdf” is the everyday choice. Only reach for “print with riso” if you’re using a risograph.

zine machine

A zine press made for creative in-the-field storytelling. Design your panels with images and words — with optional riso inks, halftones, the works — then print on letter-sized paper. Choose the one-sheet mini (eight pages, one cut, no staples) or a stapled booklet (up to 24 half-letter pages, printed double-sided). The press sheets handle all the page-shuffling math for you.

print the pdf — a ready-to-fold file for any home printer. Single-sided for the mini; double-sided, flipped on the short edge, for booklets.

fold & bind — fold and cut guides print right on the sheet, and step-by-step instructions ride along as the last page of the file.

optional: print with riso — true color separations for risograph shops: one grayscale master per ink, plus a key page naming each layer.

  1. step 1

    Print the PDF single-sided, landscape, at 100% scale — no “fit to page” shrinking.

  2. step 2

    Fold in half the long way (top edge to bottom edge). Crease hard, then unfold.

  3. step 3

    Fold in half the short way (left edge to right edge) and keep it folded.

  4. step 4

    Cut along the pink ✂ line — from the folded edge to the crease. Unfold.

  5. step 5

    Refold the long way, push both ends toward the middle — the slit opens into a diamond — then collapse it flat with your cover in front.

  1. step 1

    Print double-sided, landscape, at 100% scale. Duplex setting: flip on short edge.

  2. step 2

    Keep the sheets in printed order — sheet 1 holds the covers and goes on the outside.

  3. step 3

    Stack the sheets and fold the whole stack in half along the dashed center line.

  4. step 4

    Staple twice along the fold — long-arm stapler, or open the stack flat over a towel and press one through.

  5. step 5

    Crease hard with the back of a spoon; trim the open edge flush with a ruler and blade if you like.

Each page prints next to its faraway partner — page 1 sits beside the last page. The fold sorts them out.
